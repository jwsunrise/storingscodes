Analytics

I use an analytics service called Clicky to review how many people have visited, where they might be coming from, and what types of devices they could be using. As of June 2018, I’m looking to migrate off of Clicky and on to a service that allows for better anonymization of the data they collect. Truly, the only reason I want to know about my visitors at all is to help me understand (a) where Audacious might be getting linked to from and (b) what sort of devices I need to consider when making design changes. I don’t care about most of the other data, and — as mentioned above — I’m actively looking to move to a less accurate service.

Cloud.Typography

Audacious uses a webfont provided by Hoefler & Co. through a service called Cloud.typography. However, this service does not use cookies or JavaScript to serve the font files, and the only information collected by Cloud.typography is used to manage my billing with Hoefler & Co. For more information, you can read the terse Cloud.typography declaration, which enumerates what data they collect and why.
